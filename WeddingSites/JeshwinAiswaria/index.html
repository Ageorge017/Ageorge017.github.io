Our Story

Groom

A trip to Philly around 2011 was the first time I met Aiswaria (Nimmi as I like to call her). What was supposed to be a casual encounter between our moms who haven’t seen each other since college in approximately 30 years led to me meeting Nimmi. Aim chats became texts, texts became phone calls that lasted till 3:00 AM, which then upscaled to oovoo video calls, and our friendship grew. Once we both started college, we began our solo journeys without much contact until 2017. I decided to message Nimmi by congratulating her after seeing a dance video on Facebook that had quite a number of views. We started casually talking/checking up on each other until I decided to open up to her at St. Raymond Catholic Church in Bronx, NY. We started doing study dates together and I would fly down and visit her. We would cook together and do just about any couple things you can think of. Slowly I realized I was falling in love with Nimmi. After a couple of years around 2019, our presence in each other’s lives was such an essential factor that it did not feel right without calling and telling her good night or getting up to that good morning text/facetime. Fast forward to 2020, I asked Nimmi to marry me, and I cannot wait to see my baby boo at the altar!

Bride

The story really begins in 2011 when my mom's friends from R.A.K. Delhi nursing school came over. That's where I met Jeshwin for the first time! I remember our families planning a trip to New York and Jeshwin betting with my mom that he would eat all her spicy meen (fish) curry if she let my sisters and I go to New York with his family. To everyone's surprise, he ended up winning the bet! Soon after, he and his family went back to Texas, but we still kept in touch. This was before unlimited talk, text, and Facetime. We had to find creative ways, like oovoo, to spend time with each other, and we would stay up all night talking. Once we started college, we lost touch. Years later, he messaged me about a dance video he really liked of mine. He made an impromptu visit to NY. After that, he continued to visit me while I was finishing up with PA school. When he would visit, he would make my favorites, chicken tikka, and biryani, while I studied for my exams. Even when we were miles apart, he made it clear how much he loved and cared for me. Whether that was by sending surprise flowers or staying up all night on Facetime while I studied, he always found a way. It was his genuine love for his family, his devotion to God, and his go-getter personality that made me realize that he is the one I wanted to spend the rest of my life with. Fast forward to the beginning of 2020, he surprised me with a beautiful proposal and asked me to be his wife. Jeshwin, I am so excited to spend the rest of my life with you. Counting down the days till our forever :)
